RAJA RANI TEMPLE

The Raja Rani Temple in Bhubaneswar, built in the 11th century, is a stunning example of Kalinga architecture, crafted from red and yellow sandstone. Known as the "Love Temple," it features intricate carvings of couples and women, though it has no presiding deity. Maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India, it hosts the annual Rajarani Music Festival, celebrating Indian classical music.
